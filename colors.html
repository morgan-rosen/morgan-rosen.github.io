Reflection

Building my design system was kind of fun and kind of tricky at the same time. I went with a warm, muted palette with deep gray-green and rose tones as the foundation. To me, these colors feel grounded but still expressive, kind of like how I want my designs (and maybe myself) to feel. Then I added salmon and melon tones to bring in a bit of personality without making things feel too busy or bright. This added a little pop of fun in a calm, balanced space.

Accessibility was a big part of how I used the colors. I wanted to make sure everything was readable, so I spent time checking contrast ratios against WCAG guidelines. Darker colors like Outer Space and Paynes Gray became my go-to for body text, while lighter ones like Melon got reserved mostly for backgrounds or large text accents. It was a bit of a balancing act, I wanted the system to feel expressive and “me,” but it also functional for anyone using it.

Working on this also made me realize that while colors, type, and spacing might seem simple, having clear rules for them instantly makes a system feel cohesive. I stuck to a typography scale ( H1–H3, body, small) and consistent spacing values. Now everything feels like it belongs together, which is exactly what I wanted for my portfolio. I can see these elements carrying across different pages and giving my work a consistent, recognizable style.

In the future, I’d love to expand my fonts a bit — maybe add a different font for the smaller text while keeping this font for the headings. Overall, I’m really happy with the foundation I’ve built. It’s accessible, authentic, and feels like me. I feel like I have a system I can rely on for consistent, polished design work, but one that still leaves room for personality.
